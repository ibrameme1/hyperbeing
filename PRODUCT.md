# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Startup founders, marketers, consultants, and executives who need professional presentation decks fast — often under time pressure, with no design skills or budget for a designer. Their context: deadline in hours, a clear narrative in mind, no time to fight with PowerPoint or Figma. The job: produce a complete, visually polished deck that communicates credibly.

Secondary surface: unauthenticated visitors landing on the marketing site. Their job: quickly assess whether HyperBeing is worth signing up for before committing.

## Product Purpose

HyperBeing is an AI-powered presentation maker that turns a plain-text brief into a complete slide deck with AI-generated visuals in under 60 seconds. It exists because professional slide design has always been time-intensive and skill-gated — HyperBeing removes both barriers. Success means a user describes what they need, watches it generate in real time, makes a few edits, and exports a deck they're proud to show.

## Brand Personality

Fast, polished, effortless. Confident without being arrogant. The product demonstrates quality by doing excellent work, not by talking about it.

## Anti-references

- **ChatGPT-era dark purple slop**: Gradient text everywhere, glowing orbs as decoration, purple + cyan hype gradients used as a substitute for substance, AI buzzword copy ("supercharge your workflow"), glass cards stacked on dark backgrounds as a visual default.
- **Enterprise boring**: PowerPoint/Google Slides energy — flat, institutional, color-by-committee, zero personality, functional but forgettable.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** The product's quality is the pitch. Every part of the UI that shows the output — the slide preview, the generation stream, the viewer — must be exceptional. If the presentation it makes looks amateur, the product is dead.
2. **Speed is the product.** The experience of watching slides generate in real time is the core differentiator. Motion, progress, and streaming must feel exciting, not anxious.
3. **Effortless confidence.** The interface should never make users feel uncertain about what to do next. Every step in the brief → generate → edit → export chain should feel inevitable.
4. **Earned restraint.** Decoration must pull weight. Every gradient, glow, or visual flourish must exist because it serves a functional or emotional purpose — not because it looks "premium."
5. **Credible output.** The generated slides are the product. The UI's only job is to get out of the way and deliver them beautifully.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. No known specific user accessibility requirements, but the product serves time-pressured users across device types — keyboard-navigable flows, sufficient contrast in both light and dark modes, and mobile-responsive layouts are all required.
